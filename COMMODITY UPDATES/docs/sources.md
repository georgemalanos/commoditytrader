# Recommended free/public sources for MVP

## Core market and macro

- FRED
  Chosen because it is official, stable, and free for macro-sensitive indicators.
- U.S. EIA
  Chosen because it offers public energy balances, inventories, and official releases.
- World Bank Commodity Markets data
  Chosen because it is public and useful for contextual reference where direct exchange data is unavailable.
- IMF and central-bank calendars
  Chosen because official calendars are the cleanest source for macro event timing.

## News and public articles

- Reuters public pages and feeds where legally accessible
  Chosen because of broad market coverage and strong credibility, but should be linked and summarized carefully.
- Official company press releases
  Chosen because they are primary-source disclosures for supply, outages, and strategy changes.
- Public maritime and commodity RSS feeds
  Chosen because they provide broad coverage at zero cost, but require freshness and confidence labeling.

## Events and networking

- OPEC calendar and publications
  Chosen because energy policy timing matters directly for oil.
- Federal Reserve and ECB calendars
  Chosen because rates and FX directly affect commodity risk appetite.
- Trade association and conference organizer sites
  Chosen because they are the primary source for event details and attendance opportunities.
- Exchange calendars and inventory release pages
  Chosen because official release timing is critical for trading prep.

## Freight notes

- Public freight coverage is limited and often delayed.
- MVP therefore supports:
  - manual desk input
  - delayed or estimated public proxies
  - demo fallback content
- Baltic-linked route structures are modeled, but Baltic proprietary values are not bundled or implied.

## Source labeling system

- `Official`
  Primary institutional or regulatory source.
- `Public news`
  Reputable public reporting or RSS.
- `Public data`
  Free datasets or institutional statistics.
- `Premium`
  Licensed provider, disabled unless connected.
- `Manual`
  User or desk-maintained data.
- `Estimated`
  Proxy or delayed derived figure.
- `Demo`
  Seeded placeholder content only.
